Picture this: You're sitting at your desk, staring at a complex problem. Maybe it's a software architecture decision that's been keeping you up at night. Maybe it's a manufacturing process that needs optimization. Or perhaps you're trying to navigate the treacherous waters of enterprise compliance without drowning in bureaucracy.

Now imagine you could snap your fingers and summon **three distinct experts** to help you. Not just any experts—specialists who approach your problem from different angles, challenge each other's assumptions, and work together to deliver something far better than any single mind could create.

That's exactly what the **Consultancy Skill** brings to your terminal. ✨

## What Is This Magic?

The Consultancy Skill is a sophisticated AI-powered workflow system designed for **Kimi Code CLI**. It orchestrates three specialized AI agents—**Maker**, **Validator**, and **Aligner**—through a structured six-phase process that transforms vague ideas into polished, validated solutions.

Think of it as having a **mini consulting firm** living inside your command line. But unlike traditional consulting (where you might wait weeks for a PowerPoint deck and an invoice that makes you cry), this trio works in minutes, maintains full traceability, and never charges overtime.

### Why Does This Exist?

Let's be honest: AI assistants are powerful, but they can be... inconsistent. Sometimes they nail it on the first try. Other times, they confidently suggest solutions to problems you don't actually have, miss critical constraints you mentioned in paragraph three, or produce code that looks good but falls apart under scrutiny.

The Consultancy Skill was born from a simple observation: **Quality work requires multiple perspectives.**

Just as the best products emerge from teams with diverse viewpoints, the best AI-assisted work comes from agents that specialize in different aspects of the creative process:

- **Creation** without **verification** leads to bugs and blind spots
- **Verification** without **alignment** solves the wrong problem perfectly
- **Alignment** without **creation** is just endless meetings about meetings

The trio ensures that what gets built is not just *technically correct*, but *actually useful*.

## Meet the Trio: A Story of Three Personalities

Once upon a time in the digital realm, three agents were brought together by a wise architect who understood that great work requires creative tension. Let me introduce you to your new team:

### 🛠️ Maker — "I Build It"

The Maker is your enthusiastic builder, the one who sees a blank canvas and can't wait to start painting. They're optimistic, fast, and incredibly skilled at turning concepts into reality. When you say "I need a user authentication system," the Maker is already drafting the API endpoints before you finish your sentence.

**Maker's mantra:** *"Done is better than perfect—but let's make it pretty great anyway."*

### ✅ Validator — "I Find What's Broken"

The Validator is your skeptical quality guardian. While the Maker is busy building, the Validator is quietly asking uncomfortable questions: "What happens if the database connection fails?" "Did you consider SQL injection?" "Is this actually thread-safe?" They're not trying to be difficult—they're trying to save you from 3 AM production incidents.

**Validator's mantra:** *"Trust, but verify. Then verify again."*

### 🎯 Aligner — "I Solve the Real Need"

The Aligner is your strategic compass. They're the one who pauses the room when everyone's arguing about implementation details and asks: "Wait—why are we building this again? Who is the user? What problem are we actually solving?" They ensure that all the brilliant work actually matters to someone.

**Aligner's mantra:** *"Build the right thing, not just the thing right."*

Together, they form a complete creative ecosystem. The Maker dreams big, the Validator keeps the dream grounded in reality, and the Aligner ensures the dream is worth pursuing in the first place.

## The Consulting Process: An Analogy

To understand how the Consultancy Skill works, let's use an analogy everyone can relate to: **renovating your kitchen.**

### Phase 1: Discovery 🕵️
*"So, you want a new kitchen? Let's talk about how you actually cook."*

Before touching a single tile, the Aligner asks about your cooking habits, your storage needs, whether you entertain often, and if that vintage stove has sentimental value. The Maker sketches possibilities. The Validator checks building codes and load-bearing walls.

### Phase 2: Design Review 📐
*"Here's what we could do... and here's why we shouldn't do some of it."*

The Maker presents three layout options with stunning 3D renders. The Validator points out that the island location blocks the fire extinguisher access. The Aligner notices you never mentioned needing a coffee station, which changes everything.

### Phase 3: Build 🔨
*"Time to make it real."*

The Maker coordinates demolition, installation, and finishing. The Validator inspects electrical work and cabinet alignment. The Aligner ensures the backsplash color actually matches your vision.

### Phase 4: Validation Review 🔍
*"Let's make sure this works before we call it done."*

Every drawer is opened and closed 50 times. The oven is tested. The Validator creates a punch list of items to address.

### Phase 5: QA 🧹
*"Final polish and documentation."*

Touch-ups, cleaning, organizing manuals, and creating that "how to use your new kitchen" guide you'll actually read.

### Phase 6: Delivery 🎉
*"Welcome to your new kitchen. Here's what we built and why."*

The final handoff with full documentation, warranty information, and maybe a houseplant as a bonus.

That's exactly how the Consultancy Skill works—except instead of kitchens, you're building software, designing processes, or solving complex business problems. And instead of weeks, it takes minutes.
